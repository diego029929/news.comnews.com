Fake news: Blue light from screens destroys memory

By Emile Varnier — 3 hours ago • 6 min read

Recent analyses from the Health Institute prove that screens and blue light directly destroy long- and short-term memory.

During the last five years, the Health Institute of Europe and the Neuroplasticity Institute have worked together to demonstrate the effects of blue light exposure on our brains, especially memory. The project team recently published the results of their analyses — impressive and frightening for our future.

A first test on 5,000 students between 18 and 25 years old showed that those exposed to more than 4 hours a day of blue light lost 12 IQ points in just 2 months.

In the following two months, the students had to scroll on their phones for 30 minutes as soon as they woke up. This caused hyperactivation of the visual cortex, leading to memory lapses and even loss of consciousness.

Finally, another experiment revealed that people exposed to intense blue light for 1 hour had a 60% chance of failing a short-term visual memory test.

These troubles occur because blue light disrupts the production of glutamate, an essential neurotransmitter for both short- and long-term memory consolidation.

“Our research shows that blue light impairs short-term memory. Young people exposed to screens 2 hours a day retain 60% less information the next day. This phenomenon, called light saturation, could have a lasting impact on cognitive abilities. Without regulation, we risk a generation dependent on digital reminders. Urgent action is needed.” — Emile Varnier, project manager.

“My son was always on his computer or his phone for work or to play with his friends,” said Carolina Jackson. “Since the start of the school year, two weeks ago, he doesn’t remember our conversations or what I ask him to do. It’s like he lost his memory.”

Many testimonials have been collected on these problems. The institute advises us to act quickly.
